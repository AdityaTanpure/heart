Heart Murmur Detection System

An advanced, non-invasive diagnostic tool that analyzes Phonocardiogram (PCG) audio signals to accurately detect and classify heart murmurs using Machine Learning and Deep Learning architectures.

Pioneering Heart Health with AI

This system is developed as a dedicated academic project by the Electronics & Telecommunication Engineering department. Our goal is to bridge the gap between traditional stethoscope auscultation and modern artificial intelligence.

By extracting Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCCs) and training robust classification models, we have created a pipeline that assists in diagnosing cardiovascular abnormalities efficiently.

Why This Project Matters

What is a Heart Murmur?

A heart murmur is an abnormal sound heard during a heartbeat, often sounding like a "whooshing" or "swishing." While some are harmless, others can indicate serious underlying structural heart conditions, such as valve stenosis or regurgitation.

What is a Phonocardiogram (PCG)?

A PCG is a high-fidelity digital recording of the acoustic sounds made by the heart. By capturing audio from the four main auscultation areas (Aortic, Mitral, Pulmonary, Tricuspid), our models can visually and mathematically isolate abnormalities.

How The ML Pipeline Works

1. Audio Upload

Physicians or users upload .wav audio recordings captured via a digital stethoscope from the four primary valves. The browser generates a real-time interactive waveform.

2. Feature Extraction

The Python backend utilizes the Librosa library to convert the raw audio into Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCCs) and generates high-resolution spectrograms.

3. ML Classification

The extracted data is fed into either our Random Forest Algorithm (84.6% Accuracy) or our Convolutional Neural Network (88.9% Accuracy) to provide an instant diagnosis.
